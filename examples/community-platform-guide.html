Atlas Hub
Design principles

The community is organized around orientation, visible progress, and fast help.

The structure keeps new members from getting lost while giving moderators enough signals to intervene early.

Start with orientation

New members see only a focused welcome path until they accept rules, choose interests, and complete their profile.

Route support clearly

Questions become categorized support threads with owner, status, and resolution notes.

Recognize contribution

Roles and badges highlight helpful answers, completed projects, event participation, and mentorship.

Member journey

A five-step entry path unlocks the community without overwhelming new members.

The onboarding flow is short, explicit, and easy for moderators to audit.

InviteMember joins through an official invite and lands in the welcome area.
RulesMember reads conduct, privacy, and support expectations.
ProfileMember selects track, timezone, goals, and notification preferences.
AccessPlatform assigns roles and unlocks channels based on interests.
First actionMember posts an introduction or joins the weekly orientation room.

Governance

Moderation is documented as a service workflow, not an ad hoc reaction.

The goal is to protect members, reduce ambiguity, and make repeated decisions easier over time.

Visible rules

Rules are short, specific, and linked from onboarding, support, and reporting flows.

Private escalation

Sensitive issues move to private handling with minimal exposure and clear ownership.

Decision logs

Moderators record action, reason, owner, and follow-up without exposing private member details.

When the same question appears three times in a month, mentors convert the answer into a resource page and link it from the relevant channel.
Escalate when a report involves safety, harassment, personal data, payments, legal concerns, or repeated conduct after warning.
Each event closes with attendance, questions asked, follow-up links, replay status, and one improvement for the next session.
Launch checklist

The platform is ready when members can join, ask for help, and understand what happens next.

Launch readiness is measured by operational clarity rather than the number of channels created.

Before launch

  • Publish rules and onboarding copy.
  • Assign owners for support, events, and moderation.
  • Prepare first-week calendar and welcome prompts.
  • Test role assignment and private escalation paths.

First two weeks

  • Review unanswered questions daily.
  • Convert repeated answers into resources.
  • Track onboarding completion and first-post rate.
  • Hold a retrospective with moderators and mentors.
